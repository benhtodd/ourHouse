On occasion, you may login to our Horizon environment and discover that you are not auto-logged in to your Ubuntu Desktop. This brief article will describe the most likely cause and how to resolve the issue.

What happened?

This typically happens after you update your Ubuntu desktop.

Why?

Because the update has messed up the View Agent configuration. Don't worry, the fix isn't terribly difficult.

Gather Info

In order to resolve this issue, you'll need to re-run the agent installer from an SSH session to your desktop.

On the stuck login Screen, go ahead and Click on your name and enter your password to access your desktop.

Now, get either your desktop name (INS-CS-##) or IP address

Got it? good - now click the power button at the top right of your screen and Logout of your desktop fully

Fix The Issue

  • Login to a Windows Instructor Desktop and open Putty
  • Connect to your Desktop via putty as yourself:
    LAB\<yourusername>
  • Enter your password
  • Change to root: sudo su -
  • If necessary, unzip the VMware-horizonagent*.tar.gz file
  • Change into the folder VMware-horizonagent* 
  • Run the install_viewagent.sh script
  • type "y" when prompted and hit enter
  • If there are no errors about active sessions, reboot your VM: shutdown -r now
  • If there is an error stating unable to run due to active sessions, then you must first get your system either rebooted or logged out and then try again

Confirm Access

After re-running that install_viewagent.sh script without error and rebooting, you should now be able to log back into your Instructor Ubuntu Desktop without re-typing your password.
